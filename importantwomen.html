Christian Women in the New Testament

Name Image Scriptural References Region of Association Occupation Importance
Mary, Mother of Jesus Matthew 1:16-25; Luke 1:26-56 Nazareth, Judea Mother
Mother of Jesus, pivotal in the Incarnation, revered as a model of faith and obedience across Christian traditions.
Mary Magdalene Luke 8:2; John 20:1-18 Magdala, Galilee Follower of Jesus
First to see the risen Jesus, dubbed "Apostle to the Apostles," symbolizing redemption and loyalty.
Martha of Bethany Luke 10:38-42; John 11:1-44 Bethany, Judea Homemaker
Witnessed Lazarus’ resurrection, confessed Jesus as Messiah, exemplifying faith and service.
Joanna Luke 8:3; 24:10 Galilee Supporter of Jesus' ministry
Funded Jesus’ mission, bridged social divides, and witnessed the resurrection, aiding the Gospel’s spread.
Susanna Luke 8:3 Galilee Supporter of Jesus' ministry
Supported Jesus’ ministry financially, showing the vital role of women in His mission.
Mary of Clopas John 19:25 Judea Follower of Jesus
Stood loyally at the cross, witnessing the atonement and reflecting female faithfulness.
Anna the Prophetess Luke 2:36-38 Jerusalem Prophetess
Proclaimed Jesus as Messiah, embodying piety and prophetic insight at His presentation.
Dorcas (Tabitha) Acts 9:36-42 Joppa Seamstress, disciple
Raised by Peter, her charity and resurrection strengthened the early church in Joppa.
Lydia of Thyatira Acts 16:14-15 Philippi Merchant of purple goods
First European convert, her hospitality launched the Philippian church, advancing the Gospel westward.
Priscilla Acts 18:2-3, 18:26 Rome, Corinth, Ephesus Tentmaker, teacher
Missionary and teacher with Aquila, shaping early Christianity across Rome, Corinth, and Ephesus.
Phoebe Romans 16:1-2 Cenchreae Deaconess, benefactor (
helper of many)
Delivered Romans, a deaconess and benefactor, linking Paul to the Roman church.
Elizabeth Luke 1:5-80 Judean hill country Mother of John the Baptist
Affirmed Mary’s divine role, mother of John the Baptist, prophetically tied to Christ’s coming.
